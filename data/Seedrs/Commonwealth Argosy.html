Idea

Introduction

Commonwealth Argosy is an established international commercial initiative. It is an exclusive events and membership network, open to individuals, corporate entities and organisations interested in helping create a wealthier and more forward-looking Commonwealth.

It is a group of companies which promotes, organises and runs fee generating, bespoke events on its own account and for others. It also provides services and a sponsorship platform for its members, who pay a subscription to join. These areas of revenue centre around the Commonwealth and what we believe is its growing for trade and human capital potential.Through the Argosy Club we operate a membership based business to business service promoting networking between its subscribing members and providing networking opportunities in a structured social and professional environment.

Intended impact

Our objective is to create a global calendar of membership and sponsor-driven Commonwealth focused business events. We currently use a range of land-based venues and plan to move to a ship-based venue as a medium term objective. In the longer term a bespoke ship is planned to have a Commonwealth wide itinerary, and operate as a unique platform to promote trade and economic growth on behalf of the Commonwealth.

Shareholders who invest £500 or more will entitled to a 15% discounted membership of the Argosy Club for the duration of their shareholding. Membership will enable shareholders to become part of an exclusive network, offering discretion, professionalism, and a voice within the Commonwealth. As Members, shareholders will be invited to all the high-profile 'sponsored' events within the Argosy Calendar throughout the Commonwealth.

Shareholders who invest £2,000 or more will automatically become Members of the Argosy Club for the duration of their shareholding, with an invitation to all the 'sponsored' events throughout the Commonwealth within the Argosy Calendar.

Through a range of complementary channels and services we intend to provide an unrivalled and holistic global service available only to Commonwealth Argosy Members.

Our focus is on helping members to understand and pursue the significant growth and commercial opportunities throughout the Commonwealth.

Membership entitles access to the full Argosy Calendar of events including the invitation to host business conferences, formal dinners and receptions.

Events, conferences and receptions delivered on land and at sea will be further supported through information exchange and member to member networking through the private members’ Argosy Online site, a web-based portal which will host the online community.

In addition to Sponsorship, there are three types of membership available:
— Corporate membership
— Organisational membership
— Individual membership

Monetisation strategy

Commonwealth Argosy Ltd will initially be funded through revenue, equity and sponsorship.

We are already producing a revenue stream from land-based events and membership. We also intend to secure corporate sponsorship for major high-profile events based around specific themes, such as Resilience, Cyber Security, Health, Mining and Resources, Oil and Gas, Logistics and Education. All shareholders will be invited to these events.

As well as successful past events some of these upcoming events are in Joint Venture with highly prestigious organisations, high end brands and SMEs.

Through a robust Membership subscription model we are already delivering a series of benefits to both individual and corporate members. However, we intend to identify additional revenue streams as the company evolves. We are strongly growth orientated.

In addition we aim to have further revenue streams from advertising, the Commonwealth Resilience Forum and other JV entities, as well as annual licence fees from subsidiaries.

In the longer term, following further fund raising initiatives, Commonwealth Argosy intends to have an international ship based venue - an ocean going platform.

In March 2015, following our funding of a Report on Commonwealth Defence and Security, we launched our Commonwealth Resilience Forum. The programme for this 'live' event platform commences in January 2016.

Through a spiral revenue model we aim to provide a knowledge and networking forum for business executives, academics, and resilience practitioners. Although launched in the UK, we plan to develop the Forum into a Commonwealth-wide resilience and security network. We have secured and are actively seeking further knowledge partners and participants to create this global resilience network.

Use of proceeds

The money raised through Seedrs will be used to immediately strengthen our events programme and to leverage the Argosy Calendar, so that we can widen our offer to a global membership network and further increase revenues. It will also enable improvement to the website and development of the Argosy Online platform.

Finally, the investment will allow us to start to expand the Argosy Club franchise initially to Scotland, and then to South Africa, Kenya, and New Zealand.

The company has a total of £180,000 outstanding in interest free loans from the directors, however none of the proceeds of investment are intended to be used to repay this.

Market

Target market

Our target members are individuals, small to medium sized enterprises and private and public companies. Nevertheless we also seek customers and partners in the not for profit sectors where there is a mutual interest in sustainable bottom-up business growth.

As an international private members business club. Our home will in due course be an ocean going ship. We are exclusive not elitist; commercial not political; and free traders by instinct. We measure our success on social as well as financial profit; we believe in the responsible stewardship of our maritime environment; and our ambition is to generate more trade, more business and stronger relationships throughout the Commonwealth - creating more in 'common' and more in 'wealth'.

Characteristics of target market

The Commonwealth is our market. It is a global union of 53 countries bound together by values and aspirations. They span every habitable continent and time zone with English as its main language and Common Law - all of which help facilitate trade and business.

There is currently a shift in focus within the UK to trade opportunities beyond Europe as the Commonwealth grows rapidly in comparison to the Eurozone. In 2011, the Commonwealth generated US$1.9 trillion of (non oil) exports and contributed 13% of world exports. Of the total Commonwealth exports, 48% were contributed by the 33 developing nations, closing the divide, with 49% generated by the 6 developed nations. By some estimates, the aggregate GDP of the Commonwealth has already exceeded that of the EU, placing it third behind the US and China.

Our intention is to encourage growth in both production and supply chains in these fast developing economies through support to young entrepreneurs, SME's and private companies.

Marketing strategy

Commonwealth Argosy and its subsidiaries represent a holistic and integrated approach to promoting trade and investment opportunities via a virtual platform and high-profile live events to SME's and larger companies within and on behalf of the Commonwealth.

Argosy will focus on marketing its business to business services, corporate and private hospitality, and its high quality calendar of events to attract membership from corporates, not for profit organisations and individuals, with particular emphasis on the 'under 35' Commonwealth demographic.

The company will connect its members for business and social networking using both online and face to face communication channels. The business plans to use the following media and tactics to reach this audience:
* Advertise in high end business magazines including Centurian, Director, and industry sector trade magazines.
* Work with specific industry sectors to connect with their supply chains to mutual advantage.
* Develop a members only area on the website (Argosy Online) for business relationship building, information services and membership benefits.
*Promote the portal website.
*Continue to develop strategic partnerships with national and Commonwealth trade bodies including national chambers of commerce, B2B networks and RWHA.
* Focused marketing to corporates, SME's and private companies that have a Commonwealth footprint either through product supply chains or potential export markets.
*Grow the existing contact list.
*Create and send press and media kits with information to selected business and shipping press across the Commonwealth in a geographically sequenced timetable.
*Promote the membership and calendar of events at shipping, business and cultural forums and events.
*Sponsor 4 non-profit events per year, all chosen for the exposure they receive to the press, high net worth individuals and corporates.
* Use Argosy Foundation and its social enterprise objectives to promote the philanthropic benefits of membership.

Competition strategy

In our view there is currently no direct competition to the Commonwealth Argosy offering. To our knowledge, the architecture of the offering is unique, however, there is competition for customers in two specific segments of the target market. Firstly, there are a significant number of business networks and clubs offering similar benefits to members. These networks are either based on geographic proximity, sector alignment, or social or ethnic grouping.

The Commonwealth Enterprise and Investment Council (CWEIC), is a not for profit institution, similar to a Commonwealth UKTI. There is overlap in some areas in particular membership and trade promotion events, however, their main purpose is to support the trade pillar of the Commonwealth Secretariat by delivering the biennial Commonwealth Business Forum.

We see ourselves as being differentiated by our focus on SMEs and the breadth of our trade and economic agenda.

We consider there to be no direct competition to the comprehensive Commonwealth SME focused Argosy platform based offering. A number of trade organisations already have membership schemes similar to the Argosy model. These include the CBI, the IOD, the D Group, British Expertise and other sector specific trade bodies. However, we believe that this combination of a maritime platform, a Commonwealth brand, and a corporate membership structure is unique.

In short, there are other opportunities for corporate customers to spend in a maritime and virtual environment - Argosy is integrating both.

The underpinning to the Commonwealth Argosy is a commitment to the Commonwealth Charter and therefore the pricing structure has been calibrated in order not to create too high a barrier to entry.

An exclusively Commonwealth business to business directory we believe will appeal to members as the first of its kind - the ship platform offers prestige and cachet for both corporate customers and individuals.
