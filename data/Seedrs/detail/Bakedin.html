Idea

Introduction

Our best-selling line of mug baking mixes are based on the current trend for microwave baking, allowing you to cook a brownie, cookie or cake in a mug in less than 2 minutes.

We also sell award-winning Baking Kits, that contain all the dry ingredients needed to make amazing bakes, with handy equipment like a foil baking tin and pre-cut baking paper included.

Bakedin supplies a wide range of stockists, from independent farm shops & delis to prestigious food halls and includes Selfridges, Lakeland, Center Parcs & Amazon. Quality is paramount, with ingredients including local flour, Belgian chocolate and ground Madagascan vanilla pods.

In October 2015 we successfully funded the launch of our Baking Club subscription service using the Kickstarter platform. All of our baking kit recipes are approved exclusively for us by Michel Roux OBE, one of the world's finest pastry chefs.

Intended impact

Unless you bake regularly you’ll likely have a plethora of baking ingredients sat in your cupboard going out of date. At Bakedin we hate wasting good food; our product range solves this problem by providing just the right amounts of dry ingredients to bake fantastic cakes at home.

We have found that existing cake mixes on the market are very limited in the variety of recipes on offer, often contain unappetising processed ingredients you would never use when baking from scratch, and require you to have the correct size tins and other equipment. Our baking kits are strongly differentiated – we use good honest ingredients and pack them individually, enabling us to provide inventive, multi-stage recipes, supported by easy to follow, step-by-step instructions. For maximum convenience the kits also come with key equipment including tin, baking paper, butter measure and more. The recipes are approved exclusively for Bakedin by Michel Roux OBE.

Alongside the resurgence in popularity of traditional home baking, microwave baking has grown as a phenomenon in recent years, with numerous DIY recipes going viral on social media. However these recipes are often unreliable or indeed disastrous… we take the concept to the next level with our mug baking mixes, providing tried and tested pre-portioned mixes guaranteed to delight any palate. All you need is some butter, milk and a microwave and you can rustle up a freshly baked brownie, cake or cookie in a mug in under 2 minutes.

Subscription-based ingredient delivery services remain popular lifestyle and gifting choices, a model well-proven by companies such as HelloFresh and Gousto for main meal recipe delivery. Our baking club provides a unique baking kit each month, leveraging the power of social media to build a vibrant community around it. Our custom fitted, SALSA accredited production facility gives us the independent ability to develop and manufacture a new kit each month.

Substantial accomplishments to date

Our products are stocked by prestigious retailers including Selfridges, Lakeland, Wholefoods and Center Parcs, and are listed with leading fine food wholesaler Cotswold Fayre amongst others. We have recently been listed by Amazon and have agreement to list 6 products with a major UK retailer.

In 2014 our baking kits were recognised by the Guild of Fine Food in the Great Taste Awards, winning awards for our Chocolate Brownie Kit and Carrot & Walnut Cake Kit. In 2015 we received the same accolade for our Gluten Free Mug Brownie mix.

Featured in The Independent, The Gaurdian, The Sunday Express and The Metro. Our Baking Club subscription service was named in the Independent’s top 15 subscription gifts.

Shortlisted for the Gaurdian 2016 Small Business Awards.

Achieved SALSA accreditation in December 2015. SALSA certification is only granted to suppliers who are able to demonstrate to an auditor that they are able to produce safe and legal food and are committed to continually meeting the requirements of the SALSA standard.

Monetisation strategy

Bakedin’s products are a convenient and compelling value proposition for consumers versus the time and cost of buying retail packs of component ingredients, especially when you factor in the additional benefit of the pre-weighing and included equipment. Our custom-fitted, SALSA accredited production facility, trained staff and volume discounts enable us to operate at gross margins fitting for the industry.

We have achieved strong sales in retail outlets including farm shops, delis, garden centres and high-end food halls, supported by distribution agreements with 2 fine food wholesalers. These channels will allow us to significantly grow our base of small-to-medium sized retailers while directly pursuing contracts with large multiples and online outlets. We attend key industry trade shows to further increase our customer and distributor base, building industry contacts, and are also pursuing meetings with supermarket chains.

We also sell directly to consumers, with online channels including our own e-commerce site, and have seen strong growth in our web sales. Being able to talk directly to our customers is key, and we attend large consumer food shows to sell face-to-face and continue to build the brand. We are actively building our social media presence, and continue to target respected journalists and bloggers to support consumer awareness of, and trust in, the brand.

The launch of the Bakedin Baking Club has created a brand new sales channel, with the monthly subscription-based “unique baking kits by post” model generating a loyal repeat customer following. We will grow this business by developing a strong online sales funnel, leveraging our existing customer base and social media presence, and complementing this with a compelling referral program and gifting options.

Use of proceeds

Achieving contracts with large multiples will require us to scale our production capabilities significantly. Establishing a co-manufacturing partnership will give us added capacity on demand, but we require greater buying power to meet the minimum volumes co-manufacturers demand. Alongside this, we need to invest in our own equipment to increase the efficiency of in-house production processes, allowing us to retain agility and cut costs.

We have a number of new products in development and need to invest in new packaging formats to enable us to take them from prototype to production.

We'll increase resource in our sales team to broaden distribution of our products, including taking advantage of export opportunities.

With our large customers’ payment terms averaging 60 days, and with multi-pallet orders on the increase, we also require greater degree of working capital to cover upfront costs including bulk purchase of raw ingredients, packaging and labour costs.

**Please note, the company has outstanding directors loans of £40,930.

Market

Target market

Our consumers are predominantly female, in line with the rest of the home baking market, are affluent and not price sensitive. They have a variety of motives for purchasing BakedIn home baking goods: these include extreme convenience, a desire to explore new recipes, reduced wastage of ingredients and confidence in quality and provenance. We are currently stocked in a variety of farm shops, delis, garden centres and high-end food halls, frequented by this demographic, and believe we will see similarly strong sales when represented by quality supermarkets.

All of our products are popular gifts, extending our target market beyond those with a direct interest in baking. Sales at the BBC Good Food Show in November 2014 and 2015 were strong, with a large proportion of purchases being made for gifts and stocking fillers.

We have found that parents find all our offerings attractive: the Baking Kits allow children to bake from scratch with minimal mess or risk of failure, while Mug Brownies are a quick and fun treat.

Our microwaveable Mug Baking range aims to appeal to a much wider audience, being firmly positioned in the convenience and novelty market. Taking less than 2 minutes to cook, and the concept having been popularised by an explosion of ‘mug baking’ recipes and pictures on social media, they appeal to even the most impatient chocolate lover.

Our Baking Club appeals to individuals who do not have the time to bake routinely and are looking for a convenient way to do so. Companies like Hello Fresh and Gousto have proven this as an extremely successful model, targeting affluent professionals with a taste for cookery who are looking for variety, convenience and quality ingredients. We target the same market with our Baking Club.

Characteristics of target market

Spending in the home baking market has increased steadily over the last 5 years, with interests such as the Great British Bake Off inspiring more people to try baking at home. Viewing figures for the GBBO final rose 50% from 2013 to 2014, with 12.3m viewers – more than the World Cup Final. Yearly spend in the UK home baking market is around £1.75bn, and forecasted to remain consistent.

In our experience two of the most commonly cited reasons for baking at home are that it is seen as a relaxing activity and that encouraging children to learn to bake is important. High quality, natural ingredients rank highly amongst the most important factors when buying baking mixes. In addition, 60% of all household food waste arises from products ‘not used in time’ and there is growing desire to reduce this.

Recipe box subscription services have soared in popularity of late, with market leader Hello Fresh recently valued at $2.9bn, delivering 100,00s of meals each week.

Marketing strategy

B2B sales are a key part of our sales pipeline, and we attend all the key industry trade shows including the annual Speciality & Fine Food Fair, Food Expo, The Spring Fair and bi-annual IFE – all opportunities to meet speciality, high street and online retailers, wholesalers and exporters. This has already helped us to significantly grow our retail base and provide us with the opportunity to speak to large retailers, as well as opening up relationships with wholesalers. In addition, we have ongoing discussions with some of the largest retailers in the UK.

We currently supply a number of small, medium and large accounts direct. A large number of our small stockists purchase through our wholesalers - Cotswold Fayre and Diverse Fine Food. The broad appeal of our products mean our retailers are diverse and include farm shops, delis, garden centre, food halls, gift shops, toy shops, confectionary shops and homeware shops.

B2C sales are predominantly through own e-commerce website, with strategic focus on our Baking Club subscription. In addition we attend large consumer shows such as the BBC Good Food Show, providing us with the opportunity to talk directly to consumers and reach large volumes of people in a small amount of time to help grow the brand.

We will continue to use social media channels to reach our consumers, growing this base through the use of competitions and promotions, and will continue to pursue key industry bloggers to review our products and to trial Baking Club to boost our visibility and reputation.

We will actively build our newsletter mailing list and use this to stimulate demand through newsletters and promotions. New consumers signing up to the newsletter will be provided with instant offers to incentivise them to place their first order if they haven’t done so already. This will also be applied to Baking Club, with generous discounts offered to incentivise new subscriptions, backed up by pay per click advertising and referral bonuses.

Competition strategy

There is healthy competition in the home baking sector, ranging from cottage industries to medium-sized artisan brands. Meanwhile, large brands such as Betty Crocker and own label supermarket products offer a range of lower priced mixes.

In the endeavour to become a leading brand, from the very start of our journey we have ensured we clearly demonstrate what we believe are our main differentiators: quality ingredients, that are bagged separately in the kits, with a variety of impressive yet straightforward recipes, and handy extras provided in the kits such as the foil tin and baking paper.

There are a handful of small businesses offering similar propositions to the Baking Club, however as far as we know non have exclusive recipes approved by a chef with Michel Roux's reputation. Our significant social media following and customer base from our retail products gives us a significant advantage and places us well to dominate this side of the market.

The only direct commercial competitor to our mug baking mixes we know of is a Dr Oetker product. Our mixes are differentiated by the ingredients and taste - ours use no powdered egg or artificial flavourings, colourings or preservatives. There are no direct competitors for our gluten free offering that we know of.
