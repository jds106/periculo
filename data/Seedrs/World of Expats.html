Idea

Introduction

World of Expats aims to be the first destination for expats. We will support anyone planning to live or work abroad with the tools, services and information that they need to relocate and get the best out of their life as an expat. We will engage with expats by sharing high quality content that is personalised to the needs of each visitor, retain them as part of our community by linking them to relevant information, partners and fellow expats at every stage of their journey.

Innovative and interactive real world events – The Expat Shows - are planned to be held around the UK starting in London in September 2015 to allow would-be expats to learn from experts and from fellow expats in a positive and inviting environment.

Intended impact

The world is an ever more global marketplace and more and more people see the attractions of other parts of the world to earn a living, broaden their experience or provide an opportunity to implement their business idea. Furthermore, our experience has found that people choose to take advantage of different environments, cultures and climates to relocate abroad at every stage of their lives, whether to enjoy their freedom in their early career, bring up a family or retire.

Whatever the reason for the move, those who are planning to live abroad often face a number of challenges and many opportunities. Moving overseas can affect virtually every aspect of your life, including your family’s education and lifestyle, your financial planning, what sort of property you live in, and changes to your routine and how you interact with people.

There are many sites that cover aspects of the requirements of expats, but we feel that few provide comprehensive information and services and that they are of poor quality. World of Expats applies their knowledge of the expat lifestyle with high quality and relevant content delivered using the information that we capture about expats and innovative techniques to ensure that people only receive information and services that are directly relevant to their needs.

Everything that we do online, in newsletters and magazines and at events is designed to make expats feel a part of a community that is relevant to their needs. By maintaining involvement with the community, we will continue to provide access to relevant information, tools and services at every stage of their expat journey so that they see World of Expats as the first destination for their needs as an expat.

Substantial accomplishments to date

World of expats has built two websites, www.worldofexpats.com and www.expatnetwork.com. World of Expats has over 5,000 pages of content covering 180 destinations. The Expat Network portal provides a jobsboard, contract news and advice. It is number one on Google for Expat Jobs with a very active and growing community (database of 112,000 expat contractors and over 10,000 in a LinkedIn group) and attracts over 50,000 visitors a month (90,000 in August).

We held our inaugural event, The Expat Show, at London Olympia in September. The Times and The Sunday Times were our media partners; Rightmove Overseas, Silversurfers were cornerstone sponsors and Australian Futures, the Association of International Property Professionals and other key partners participated in the event.

Monetisation strategy

The objective is to engage with expats at the point that they are planning to become an expat through our portals and events. Whether they are looking to find a new role or set up a business abroad, buy a property for their leisure or as part of their retirement abroad, we will provide them with the information and access to the tools and services needed to achieve their goal.

We will engage with them as they plan their expat life through membership of clubs and communities relevant to their needs so that we can develop a long term relationship.

We will then assist them with their relocation by giving them personalised access to all of the services they require online. We will support them in getting the best out of their life as an expat, dealing with the special challenges and opportunities of the cross-jurisdictional and cross-cultural existence.

Revenues generated will include:
• Advertising, affinity and referral fees
• Membership and subscription fees
• Recruitment packages
• Sponsorship and exhibitor revenues from events

Online revenue growth will be driven by the growth in traffic to the sites and engagement with the audience. We expect event revenue to grow as events become established and from planned geographic expansion as we replicate them in other venues across the UK. We also believe that there will be scope to develop other income streams, including franchising different locations, developing new event formats and moving into services and events for the corporate sector.

Use of proceeds

The funds raised will be used to continue building Expat Network and enhance functionality to broaden into new recruitment sectors and provide wider services to the growing community.

It will also be used to drive the growth of World of Expats, taking advantage of the revenue generating partnerships already in place. We intend to fund the development of an upgraded portal to improve SEO and the customer journey and then finance digital marketing activity to build awareness alongside the activities already planned and underway.

We want to implement an innovative database structure to achieve advanced management of membership across the two sites and facilitate the use of advanced CRM tools to deliver personalisation of content and communications.

The funds will also provide greater working capital to support the development of the business.

The Company currently has outstanding director loans of £11,500; the proceeds of this investment will not be applied towards repaying the debt.

Market

Target market

World of Expats is looking to service the market of those living and working abroad. This includes those seeking to work or take up a contract abroad, those looking to emigrate long term and those looking to buy a first or second home abroad or retire abroad.

In an increasingly global world the number of people living and working abroad is rising. There was a 25% growth in international assignees in the decade to 2011 and PwC predict a further 50% growth in mobile employees by 2020.

Approximately five million Britons and over six million Americans live abroad. There are over 50 million people living outside their home country. All generations are affected with 84% of UK and 72% of US students wanting to work abroad and one in twelve people of retirement age in the UK living overseas, with over one million receiving their UK pensions abroad. Retirement abroad is less prevalent in the US, but 550,000 Americans receive their social security benefits abroad and we expect that this will increase as people seek to avoid US healthcare and other costs.

We believe that a wide range of service providers is increasingly conscious of and keen to access this growing market. World of Expats will target this market, focusing on inbound and outbound from the UK and US.

Characteristics of target market

The expat market continues to grow as globalisation increases the desire and need to live abroad. Living abroad involves a ‘re-birth’, as almost every aspect of an expat’s life is reorganised and re-equipped.

The market is changing and growing:
• Ever greater population living outside their home country:
– 50 million worldwide
– Over five million Britons and six million Americans
- Over 280,000 leave the UK each year

• 74% of expats are individual workers
– 31% earn over £100k (11% over £200k)
– 25% growth in assignments over last decade (50% growth by 2020)
– 84% of UK students want to work abroad (72% in US)

• Overseas property purchases increasing
– We believe there is growing demand as part of lifestyle change with Euro exchange rate, low prices and new annuity rules driving UK demand

• Over 50s changing lifestyle as part of retirement planning:
– One million Brits receive pension abroad (550,000 Americans)

Marketing strategy

Our marketing strategy is to build strong brand awareness in the UK and US through effective digital marketing and PR strategies and by leveraging partnerships with leaders in relevant sectors.

Our approach to marketing and PR would include:

– SEO –
• Our sites will be optimised to ensure that they gain and maintain high search engine ranking.
• Established techniques will be used to ensure that content on partner sites builds awareness of our sites and events and to improve our standing on Google.

– Online and offline advertising –
• PPC search ads on Google
• PPC on Twitter & LinkedIn
• Offline advertising in relevant sector magazines and publications

– Social media –
• We are and will continue to be active on Twitter, Facebook, Google+ & LinkedIn feeds covering similar issues as the blogs above.
• We will build communities on these platforms to drive revenue directly and link them to our websites.

– Partnerships –
• We have established partnerships with a number of high volume sites that have relevant audiences (Rightmove Overseas, Silversurfers, Mumsnet). We intend to use these partnerships to promote our sites and events and identify new partnerships to share content and build awareness.
• The Expat Show has given us the opportunity to establish a media partnership with The Times and The Sunday Times. We want to build on this relationship to raise awareness of our events and the site in the UK. Similar arrangements are planned to be established in the US when we begin to run events there.

– PR –
We will use PR to establish World of Expats as the ‘go to’ source of information on issues around living and working abroad. By releasing regular articles and press releases we want to ensure that we achieve good coverage of the issues addressed by our sites and events. We will wherever possible identify opportunities for joint PR with the partnerships we have and will establish.

Competition strategy

There are a number of websites set up to provide information or services to different sectors of the expat market. Many portals serve particular destinations (e.g. Singapore, Dubai, Spain etc) or particular segments of the market (Property Buyers, International Assignees etc). The sites providing access to a wider range of information, tools and services are of variable quality and are generally limited in the quality of content and range of information, tools and services provided. We found that the competition is fairly fragmented with no dominant player.

To our knowledge nobody in the market is covering a full range of destination information for all types of expat and providing the comprehensive range of information, tools and services as envisaged by World of Expats. By linking the information to a series of major consumer shows around the UK (and later in the US) we will engage with people as they are planning their move and give access to these people to high quality service providers.

World of Expats aims to differentiate itself by:
• Providing information that draws on our experience having lived as expats ourselves, combined with expert input from quality service providers and industry thought leaders.
• Creating and using the data captured to ensure that visitors to the sites as well as recipients of newsletters and mailings receive only information and services relevant to them.
• The quality and range of information, tools and services given in helping people to relocate and get the best out of their life as an expat. We will use quality ratings from users to monitor the quality of the service provided by those we promote on the site.
• We will establish The Expat Show as the brand associated with innovative events for expats providing an entertaining and informative day for anyone planning their life abroad.
