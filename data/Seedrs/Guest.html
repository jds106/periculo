Idea

Introduction

We plan to create the world's best cocktail bar, but in a city spoilt by the most innovative and multi-award winning venues, how does a new food and drink operation distinguish themselves?

Any venue is only as strong as its weakest link, so our solution is simple–don’t have any. We will only have the best staff in the industry - current/former head bartenders and bar managers, General Managers, brand reps and ambassadors. We plan to obtain this level of staff by only hiring each person on a day-to-day ‘GUEST SHIFT’ basis. These creative individuals will bring their skill and flair to create the most exciting cocktails in the world.

Expanding upon the idea, we want to engage with pop-up operators, food truck cooks, and junior chefs from Michelin-star restaurants to develop our food offering.

We also feel that the live music venues in London have fallen short in matching their music bookings with their service, food and drink offering.

The idea is simple; use the best to get the best.

Intended impact

With so much choice in London, cocktail drinkers often have to go to many different areas and bars to get the best of the London cocktail scene. But having the best bartenders from across London and the world means that customers can be ensured that each time they visit Guest, they will be sampling the pinnacle of cocktail making.

We plan to supplement this experience by offering different and complementing food to encourage the customers to spend their entire evening at Guest. An evening made, even more, unique by including live music.

Substantial accomplishments to date

Besides the founders combined several years worth of experience building up their knowledge and expertise in order to create Guest, we have set in place many elements of what we will need in order to push the button.

We have been in active interaction with leading commercial agents, not only specifying and visiting sites that match our requirements but also creating a pitch deck for landlords. In a world of franchises, it is important for independents to have landlords onside. We have identified three sites on Broadwick Street which we are considering.

Most importantly, we have quietly been approaching the best cocktail personnel we know and have built up our first two months rota. Without giving too much away, we feel the lineup is really impressive.

We have begun the all important procurement meetings with alcohol brands and have garnered an impressive reaction from all of the major players, this will ensure that we have cost-cutting methods in place before we even start.

Monetisation strategy

Our main revenue stream will be through the sale from drink and food. We plan on an average 60 - 75% margin on drinks and food. In our view, the two deciding factors on maximising revenue for F&B businesses are firstly the idea and secondly capacity. Popular venues have a certain plateau and therefore, the larger the site the better daily take. With that in mind, we are looking for a larger than usual 150 capacity venue.

As we will be a live music venue we will always have music at the venue, but we plan to have special events for famous musicians or festivals. We plan to capitalise on the venue's position and size in order to offer record labels and PR companies the opportunity to rent out the space for album launches or special events.

Our special ‘Guest Who?’ events will feature key industry personnel delivering master classes, demonstrations, and tasting sessions. These will include Master Blenders, Distillers, Winemakers, Brewers, and other esteemed industry royalty.

London Cocktail Week hits the capital every year in October and is an absolute plethora of tastings, seminars, master classes and events that inspire and attract not only bartenders and industry professionals but hundreds of interested people who work in other areas from all over the country and abroad.

We see it as a shame that this only lasts a week, as it is an incredible time for the industry, and it is our aim to bring a taste of this to every week across the year at Guest.

Guest will also operate a membership whereby our members get specials on drinks, and priority access to events. Guest will be open to everyone until midnight, whereby after that only members and their guests are allowed to continue the fun with us. This exclusivity gives our member’s a much-prized late venue, helps with our licencing and also gives the company an additional sales avenue.

Lastly, we will look to use the food and cocktail recipes generated by our guests over time to exploit various publishing options.

Use of proceeds

With the money raised on Seedrs we will look to:

◆ Place a deposit to secure a venue for the bar
◆ Refurb and fit out the venue
◆ Start international PR campaign

Market

Target market

The hospitality industry has always been a prominent one but in recent years has significantly flourished. The ‘wet’ side of the business has come on leaps and bounds, as the average consumer learns and adapts their palates’ to a broader and sometimes more sophisticated offering.

In our view, a very high standard has been set and our average consumer is now for the first time really starting to understand, appreciate and even expect a higher level of service and product to be presented to them.

According to 2014 William Grant & Sons Report, the on-trade spirits market is worth £5.4 billion, whilst 79% of all drinkers consider themselves spirit drinkers.
The CGA Strategy cocktail market report records a 10.8% increase of cocktail sales in the last two years of 2 year.

The Q2 2015 report from CGA Strategy shows that 52% of women and 35% of men are cocktail drinkers. In the same report, it is stated that drink led bars have enjoyed a 2.7% like for like boost since the beginning of 2014.

More generally our other target markets include:

◆ Dating couples between the ages of 25 to 55 looking for an impressive alternative date experience with excellent food, drinks and service with a live performance element.

◆ Event Organisers wanting a well-balanced event deals and packages with a tailored experience.

◆ Jazz and blues enthusiasts who are actively pursuing the best venues which offer a live performance program that is well curated. Whilst being catered to with the best drinks, food and service.

Characteristics of target market

London has been a prominent player in the global cocktail scene and is currently leading the Top 5 of The World's 50 Best Bars. London is today certainly regarded as one of the global leaders in the cocktail scene, with bartenders from all over the world coming to work here. Trends are set in London and then followed/copied by other cities like New York, Paris, Berlin, and Moscow etc.

As far as we know the entire cocktail industry in London is influenced and shaped primarily by no more than 10 cocktail bars, and a select few industry professionals. This would break down to a maximum of 5 influential people per bar, and then around 20 other industry professionals. This is approximately 70 people in London who we believe are realistically driving and controlling the direction of this industry. We feel that Andrew is part of this scene and we expect this wider group to form the core of our rotating staff at GUEST.

Marketing strategy

We intend to work closely with UK and international PR agencies to get our message across on the press and social media.

Furthermore, we plan to tap into the each guest's extensive following in order to interact directly with their fans.

As many of the practitioners are also brand ambassadors, we aim to leverage their brand's considerable marketing budgets and networks to access even more customers.

As with all major hospitality launches, we aim to harness the initial buzz of our opening few months by continuing with rotating menus and staff. We believe this can only be achieved by effectively communicating who is on each week via our website, social media and, in the case of very famous guests, the press.

Competition strategy

As we are embracing the cocktail scene rather than competing against the established players, our success is closely linked to our competitors success.

If a competitor wins awards, then their bartenders that we use from them, only makes our offering stronger.

We will always credit each establishment, each of guest come from, as much as many of them might advertise that their staff are guesting at us. This two-way complimentary marketing has the potential to help everyone as it gets more people exposed to the cocktail scene. Eventually, we hope to create the scenario whereby doing a shift at Guest is seen as "badge of honour" whereby competitors will actively encourage their staff to get a guest shift at us.
