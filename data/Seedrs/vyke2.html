Idea

Introduction

We use many different email addresses and social profiles to help manage the different parts of our modern lives, yet make do with just one mobile number.

In January 2016, Vyke launched a new app that provides the innovation our mobiles are crying out for, but mobile operators don't offer.

With Vyke, you can now add up to 4 additional UK, USA, Canadian, Dutch or Polish mobile numbers, get free peer-to-peer chat features and make low cost international calls - all from the convenience of a simple, easy to use iOS or Android app.

Vyke has been created as a B2C brand, a white label option and also for other large global brands to incorporate our calling features into their own core apps.

In response to feedback from Seedrs investors, Vyke has lowered its pre-raise valuation from £2.93m to £2m. We have restarted the campaign to offer a new opportunity to invest in our exciting future.

Intended impact

Providing A New Layer of Mobile Security and Control.

Make and receive calls and SMS from any of your mobile profiles. You can see who’s calling you, and on which line - so you can decide whether to take the call or bounce it to voicemail.

Protect the identity of your main mobile number - ideal for dating or selling stuff online. Add to this the freedom to talk, message and share with other Vyke users for free, or with anyone else anywhere in the world, at incredibly low rates.

It’s easy and free* to create a new mobile profile. Each number comes with its own custom voicemail, availability controls and personal avatar identity.

*The first UK number is offered for free.

Vyke is a one-stop shop of many of the great features offered in Skype, WhatsApp, Snapchat, Burner and Hushed but with much more.

Vyke trades under a variety of brands that all utilize the same Vyke VoIP platform;

Vyke Lite.
A simple app-to-PSTN service, this brand will be closed following the user base migration to Vyke in March 2016.

VykeBusiness.
SME users base. Features will be added as a “Vyke Pro” premium feature set in Q2 2016. VykeBusiness users will be migrated and then the brand will be closed.

Phoneserve.
Legacy pre-paid calling card & apps. This market is in decline and Vyke anticipates exiting this market within the next 12 months.

3rd Party termination.
Call termination contract with Nimbuzz Out!

This raise is for the launch and marketing of Vyke only, and not to support any of the existing brands owned by the company.

Substantial accomplishments to date

- £936k revenue in 2015.*

- Vyke was one of the first mobile VoIP providers in the world, with apps integrated in Nokia handsets as early as 2007.

- Microsoft used Vyke for a MSN Messenger dial out feature.

- Vyke provides Nimbuzz and its 200+ million users with high quality PSTN termination.

- VykeBusiness was introduced in 2015 with admin and billing control for business usage, in order to cut down on the cost of roaming and international calls.

- New Vyke app launched on 29th January 2016.

- First white label deal signed 8th February 2016.

*Source; Unaudited management accounts.

Monetisation strategy

Vyke intends to earn revenues from 'free' use, number subscriptions plus outbound & inbound calls. The rich peer-to-peer chat features are not directly monetised, but will serve to attract new users to the service.

The commercial model is made up of 4 core components;

1. Outbound call and SMS termination revenues.
- Revenue creation through charging for outbound calls and SMS made from any Vyke number.
- Focus on domestic traffic.

2. Mobile number subscriptions.
- Strong commercial propositions and low price points.
- 1st UK number will be free, all other numbers are 99p per month.
- Vyke numbers reduce to 58p (12 month advance payment of £6.99).

3. Termination Revenues.
- Vyke receives operator payments to terminate calls and SMS to Vyke numbers.
- Vyke can make indirect revenues from customers without taking payments.

4. White label & SDK fees.
- £25k to £60k setup fees, subject to deployment complexity.
- Monthly "per user" charges with monthly minimums.
- Wholesale termination charges or switch transit fees.
- Call and SMS termination revenue share.

Market

Target market

There are 6 core user types that will benefit from Vyke’s unique feature set;

1. Multiple Device Users.
The UK alone, 30m+ people have more than one mobile number so carry additional mobiles. Users can either create new numbers or port in existing personal or work numbers, then ditch their additional devices.

2. International Travellers.
Unlike regular mobile numbers, Vyke numbers are not geographically restricted, so users can make and receive calls and SMS from anywhere and therefore avoid costly roaming charges when travelling around the globe.

3. Domestic Users.
Vyke now appeals beyond it's traditional migrant base into a much larger domestic market as users can create multiple personas to help them manage, secure and control the different parts of their day-to-day mobile lives. Competing directly with MNO's and MVNO's, Vyke offers similar pricing with features operators cannot offer.

4. Ex-Pats.
Nationals living abroad can use Vyke numbers to make and receive calls just as if they were at home.

5. iPads/Tablet Users.
Turns a tablet into a fully functioning mobile phone without the need for a mobile GSM SIM card.

6. Virtual Presence.
Have a presence in another country other than where you are. Great for receiving international sales calls.


Territories.
Vyke has launched with UK, US, Canadian, Dutch and Polish “in-bundle” mobile numbers, meaning calls to a Vyke number come from inclusive minutes bundles, if applicable and are not charged at a premium rate.

Channels;
1. Vyke’s existing B2C market.
2. Vyke’s White label B2B2C proposition - either fully managed end-to-end or via custom API deployments.
3. Exclusive partnership with X-Mobility to supply a white label version of Vyke for X-Mobility to distribute to its global customer base on a revenue share basis.

Characteristics of target market

Characteristics of target market.

Infonetics Research: The mobile VoIP OTT market is forecast to grow from 1.5 billion users in 2015 to 3 billion global users in 2017, as users adopt more innovative service offerings from disruptive OTT providers.

Juniper Research believes that mobile network operators will generate revenues of close to $20 billion by 2018.

This important change in the market will be driven by improvements in network technology, increased competition and the move by Operators to join the OTT space.

From Grandview Research report:
Mobile VoIP has been gaining subscribers all over the world; growth in developed countries has been significant. Developing countries are expected to be the new high growth potential markets. In North America and Europe, Mobile VoIP has been a key focus area. Asia Pacific is expected to be a high growth potential region, expected to concentrate more on wide area communication using 3G/4G protocols.

Marketing strategy

Vyke will have 3 core channels to market;

1. B2C.
Vyke has migrated 10k existing Vyke "Lite" users to Vyke during early March 2016, followed up with a £191k launch campaign consisting of.

Print campaign.
- Metro Newspaper A5 adverts.

Digital campaign.
- PPC (primarily Adwords, with small Facebook and Twitter trials).
- Classifieds – Gumtree, Loot, Auto Trader, etc.
- Dating sites – Match.com, etc.
- Travel sites – TripAdvisor, Expedia, Booking.com, etc.

PR & Social.
- Commission YouGov research to generate stories from research:
- People with multiple mobile phones.
- Phone number privacy issues (with case studies illustrating the need for privacy).
- Roaming behaviours and attitudes.

The average cost of acquisition is budgeted at £6.50 per 4:1 paying: non-paying users. Vyke is also assuming 4 additional users through viral ‘Invite’ distribution.

2. White Label deployments.

Vyke is offered directly and indirectly as a fully managed "cookie cut" white label service.

B2B2C - Partnership with X-Mobility.
Vyke will provide X-Mobility’s with an exclusive white label OTT application as a vehicle for X-Mobility to sell their numbers to their own clients. Vyke will earn revenues through monthly user fees and call and SMS termination revenues.

B2B2C - Direct.
Vyke will sell white labels to brands wishing to launch their own service or incorporate Vyke features into their own branded apps, using Vyke's SDK/API's. The pipeline will be focussed towards the international Travel and Dating sectors.

Each solution can be adapted to incorporate additional features to suit individual requirements for additional development fees.

Competition strategy

Competitive proposition.

Vyke has secured favourable commercial terms for the supply of UK mobile numbers in return for co-developing a white label app for with X-Mobility.

Vyke is confident that it will be able to offer free UK mobile numbers and make positive revenues from outbound wholesale calls, SMS margins and inbound termination commissions.

Feature Roadmap.

Vyke intends to continue to evolve the core feature set during 2016 to include;

- VykePro – service offering for business to manage employee accounts through a simple admin interface and single pre-pay or post-pay bill.

- Free seamless Wi-Fi – Worldwide seamless Wi-Fi hotspot connectivity.

- Video – adding Video conference calling in H2 2016.

- 4G Data – Adding Vyke branded data SIM bundles through our relationship with X-Mobility.

- Collaboration features to make it easier to share data.

First mover advantage.
Vyke is a leading global product to offer a persona based multiple mobile number product and believes it's in a great position for 1st mover land grab.

Loyalty.
The nature of dedicated mobile numbers is inherently sticky, so Vyke envisages a low active user churn rate (9% per month), making it less likely for users to adopt future competitor offerings. This logic is also applied to free peer-to-peer use.
