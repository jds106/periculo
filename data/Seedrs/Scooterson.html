Idea

Introduction

Scooterson is an intelligent electric kick-scooter. Using a unique combination of cloud and smartphone technology alongside big data analysis, it is designed to understand and enhance the rider’s experience, whilst maximizing energy efficiency and safety.

Scooterson has a smart feature that allows riding without an acceleration lever and gear changer. The intelligent speed control, simply understands how fast to go by analysing the rider’s body moves. Using BigData, it understands rider’s daily habits and can predict and suggest likely destinations. With use, Predictive Routing becomes increasingly accurate and will be used by other smart features to increase energy efficiency and safety, based on riding patterns, rider profile and surrounding terrain.

The Scooterson app allows the scooter to be shared with family, friends and co-workers. All it’s users can have powertrain settings and hierarchical privileges over the ownership of the vehicle.

Intended impact

In the “Internet of Things” era, Scooterson's mission is to develop innovative electric vehicles that use mobile and cloud-connected technology to enhance control, experience and inspire the adoption of sustainable personal transportation.

Scooterson products are beautifully crafted to meet the market demand for minimalist, aesthetically pleasing products and to give each Scooterson owner a strong sense of individuality.

One of our goals is to encourage sustainable personal transportation and one of the ways to achieve this is by sharing resources. Through the Scooterson App, owners can share their vehicle with family, friends and co-workers. Using the app, owners can park the vehicle anywhere around town, to be found and used by other co-owners.

To enhance the experience further, we will develop the Scooterson City Ecosystem, a collection of services accessed through the Scooterson App. One of the components will be a network of rider friendly venues around the city: co-working spaces, pubs, bars, restaurants and other venues, where Scooterson owners can plug-in to charge their battery or switch it with a fully-charged one.

One of the most exciting planned features of our network will be the on-demand battery switching service. Through the Scooterson App, a rider would be able to hail for a battery switch, anywhere in the covered area, and a courier will bring a charged battery in less than 15 minutes.

Another interesting planned component of Scooterson City Ecosystem is the on-demand valet service. When an owner has a change of plans, a valet can be hailed through the app, to pick-up the vehicle then and drive it home or to another location, with the option of having the scooter cleaned and charged.

Scooterson City Ecosystem would be available as SaaS or Paas solution to other electric vehicles manufacturers.

Scooterson is reimagining the two-wheeled vehicle for the 21st century, ushering in a new age of connectivity and fun.

Substantial accomplishments to date

• The first prototype was built in the summer of 2013 as a proof-of-concept and we tested the ergonomics and feeling of riding.

• Based on our tests, in 2014, a new design was developed on computer-aided design (CAD) software.

• In spring 2015, two prototypes were finished and sent for heavy testing. These prototypes are now mechanically fully developed and tested.

• The tooling for the composite parts is built and ready for production.

• The tooling for assembling the frame is under development.

• The software is under development, currently in alpha stage.

• In October 2015, we ran a test to gauge market interest in our product. Almost 400 customers subscribed on our landing page, to be the first in-line for pre-orders. These customers were acquired in a short timeframe of a few days, when the landing page was open for a limited period. The traffic was driven by a test article we sent to a small number of bloggers.

• We have secured a Community Trademark for word "SCOOTERSON" and a Registered Community Design for the design of our scooter. Both I.P. are registered through Office for Harmonization of the Internal Market and covers all EU member states.

Monetisation strategy

Scooterson's business model relies on five future revenue streams:

1. Scooterson would generate revenue through the sale of its products, to individuals (B2C) and corporate customers (B2B). The Scooterson portfolio currently consists of two products, Rolley, a premium model and Rolley+ , a high-end model. These models will be followed by a luxury model, then by an economy model, both currently at the inception stage.

2. Subscription fees or hourly charged access to Scooterson City Ecosystem, the on demand service for Scooterson users.

3. Revenue through rentals of scooters using a model similar to ZipCar or Scoot Networks.

4. Subscription fees for access to Scooterson SaaS, for other electric vehicles manufacturers who want to implement similar smart functions into their products.

5. Licensing fees from selling PaaS platform to corporate customers.

Market

Target market

Scooterson’s mission is to build intelligent vehicles that inspire consumers to adopt sustainable forms of transport, without compromising on style.

The Light Electric Vehicle global industry is already a market worth billions of pounds and is projected to grow rapidly over the coming years, as electric vehicles continue to enter the mainstream consciousness.

The primary target audience of Scooterson are young, affluent professionals. They are early adopters that are highly invested in making a personal lifestyle statement. The characteristics of our primary consumer base are as follows:
- Are 25-45 years old, both male and female
- Live in central metropolitan areas
- Have a university degree or higher
- Earn £50,000 per year or above
- Are tech savvy and early adopters
- Are invested (both emotionally and financially) in their personal lifestyle
- Have an active social life
- Make efforts to lead an environmentally-friendly lifestyle.

The second target market comprises of corporate clients with extensive premises that require employees, security or clients to travel large distances over the course of their working day. These clients might currently use Segways or bikes onsite. This secondary market includes:
- Corporate campuses, University campuses
- Industrial parks and platforms, large warehouses
- Trade show centres
- Commercial centres and retail outlets
- TV media centres and film studios
- Sports stadiums and golf courses.

The third market is for companies and city councils in the sector of rentals and/or the sharing economy, particularly focusing on the following outdoor areas:
- Pedestrian zones and parks
- Tourist attractions and routes
- Beaches and coastal areas.

Characteristics of target market

Light Electric Vehicles (LEVs) comprise one of the largest and fastest-growing electric vehicle markets.

The combination of climate change concern, growing urbanisation and congestion on the roads is encouraging consumers towards sustainable forms of transportation, increasing the use of two-wheeled vehicles. As more people in developed countries choose to live in urban centres, we believe LEVs are set to become a popular choice of personal transportation.

Following the clean-tech and sustainable living movements, we feel that young professionals will contribute significantly to the growth of the global LEV market as they choose e-bicycles and e-scooters as their preferred form of transport. As a consequence, electric vehicles will become increasingly mainstream.

According to Navigant Research, combined worldwide sales of e-motorcycles, e-scooters and e-bikes are expected to grow from 37 million units annually in 2014, to nearly 46.2 million units by 2024.

Marketing strategy

Scooterson intend to approach the market using the following communications strategy:

1. Online campaigns released via social media channels will focus on visual content (photo and video).

2. Gaining coverage and reviews from top tier internet publications and influential bloggers. Our strategy will include both organic and paid articles.

3. Organising test-ride tours in major city centres where potential clients can test and experience the scooters. Alongside this city centre strategy, test-rides will also be offered outside shopping centres and other appropriate venues.

4. Celebrity endorsement through product placement in music videos and other media. For broad exposure the focus will be on publishing this content via celebrities’ social-media channels.

5. Attending top conferences, shows and fairs.

6. Word-of-mouth marketing. Emphasis will be placed on maintaining customer excitement with new experiences and improved features revealed in each software upgrade. This combined with the app and product experience, is expected to create brand loyalty and a community of customers likely to recommend Scooterson to their friends and co-workers.

Driven by our communications strategy described above we will achieve direct sales through our website. Using mobile point of sale (POS) technology, customers will also be able to pay in person for our products following a test-ride.

Competition strategy

Scooterson’s key competitive advantages over other electric scooters/kickscooters are:

Easy and Intuitive Riding - Scooterson is designed with a smart feature that allows it to be ridden without an acceleration lever and gear changer. This means that the scooters are intuitive and easy to use, creating a great first impression to a potential buyer during a test-ride with the aim to encouraging impulse purchases.

Smart and Connected - Scooterson is one of the few vehicle in its size category that has 'smart' features and is connected to a cloud-network through a smartphone.

One of our competitors advertise a scooter with a few similar smart features. However, its size and weight place it in the Vespa-style category, requiring proper riding skills, making it less attractive for inexperienced riders.

Attractive Design – A product’s design is crucial to attract interest and leave a lasting impression. With a few exceptions, most of the available electric light two-wheeled vehicles are designed in China and perceived to be “ugly”, “clunky-looking” or just “awkward” by our target market. With a minimalist and timeless design that mixes classic and modern trends, Scooterson will look great over the years, whilst also making a strong statement of individuality.

Energy Efficient - Power consumption costs are anticipated to be under ₤1 for 100Km (60 miles), this is already good but what makes Scooterson unique in the market is that the range and performance will improve as Scooterson "learns" about its owner’s riding habits.
